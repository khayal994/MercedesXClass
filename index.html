Mercedes X Class:

With pick-up trucks proving popular with buyers, it wasn't going to be long before a premium manufacturer put one on sale. The Mercedes X-Class arrived towards the end of 2017, and it delivers something different for

pick-up truck and SUV buyers alike. Those in the know will be aware that the X-Class shares its platform with the Nissan Navara, but to dismiss the X-Class as merely being a Navara with a three-pointed star on the nose and a model-for-model price hike of around £7k just demonstrates that those critics haven't driven

and close The smoother looks outside are one thing, but Mercedes has also added a premium interior inside, especially if you go for one of the higher spec variants. This gives the X-Class an upmarket feel that even puts the company's ageing GLE-Class SUV to shame, while the level of tech and equipment on board is competitive We have stopped global production for this one-of-a-kind, double-cab, and all new vehicle stock in South Africa is sold

We will, however, continue providing PremuimDrive customers Mercedes-Benz X-Class for the duration of their contract period and further parts supply will be available for at least 10 years. The Mercedes-Benz X-Class (W470) is a luxury pickup truck that was sold by the German automaker Mercedes-Benz, a division of German multinational company Daimler

Unveiled at a world premiere in Cape Town, South Africa in July 2017, the pickup took its chassis from the Nissan Navara and employed many Mercedes-specific features and technologies, including some engines not shared with the Navara.

It first went on sale in late 2017. Production started in 2017 at the Nissan factory in Spain.[6] Plans to produce the pickup at the Renault plant in Santa Isabel, Argentina were cancelled due to alleged contractual disputes between executives from both marques.
